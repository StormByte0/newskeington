#addition

**Purpose:** Long-term dependencies.

Unlike status effects, addictions evolve gradually and create their own gameplay loops.

Each addiction should probably share the same internal structure.

Example:

```js
alcohol = {
    tolerance,
    dependence,
    withdrawal
}
```

**Examples**

* Alcohol
* Caffeine
* Nicotine
* Sleeping Pills

**Changes**

* Repeated use.
* Abstinence.

**Used by**

* Automatic dialogue.
* Willpower expenditure.
* Status generation.

#body


**Purpose:** The player's physical body.

Represents the underlying body, not clothing.

Should change slowly.

**Examples**

* Chest
* Waist
* Hips
* Shoulders
* Muscle
* Body Fat
* Skin
* Hair Length
* Hair Quality
* Hormone Levels
* Voice Pitch

**Changes**

* Diet.
* Exercise.
* Hormones.
* Time.

**Used by**

* Appearance generation.
* Clothing fit.
* Certain dialogue.
* Medical events.

#conditioning


**Purpose:** Long-term behavioral conditioning.

This is different from addiction.

Addiction is physiological.

Conditioning is learned behavior.

**Examples**

* Obedience
* Assertiveness
* Professionalism
* Dependency
* Initiative
* Confidence

**Changes**

* Company policies.
* Repetition.
* Promotions.
* Certain story events.

**Used by**

* Dialogue availability.
* Automatic decisions.
* Branching routes.
* Character narration.

These fluctuate constantly during gameplay.

**Examples**

* Stress

#stats

**Purpose:** Innate character capability.

These are your classic RPG attributes. They don't directly perform actions, they influence calculations.

**Examples**

* Strength
* Dexterity
* Constitution
* Intelligence
* Wisdom
* Charisma

**Changes**

* Through practice
* Through major events, training, permanent effects.

**Used by**

* Skill checks.
* Job performance modifiers.
* Dialogue.
* Unlocking events.

#status


**Purpose:** Temporary conditions.

These are generated by everything else.

They don't own gameplay.

They **modify** gameplay.

**Examples**

* Hungover
* Restrictive Bra
* Blisters
* Exhausted
* Sick
* Drunk
* Withdrawal
* Sunburn

**Changes**

* Frequently.

**Used by**

* Stat modifiers.
* Passage conditions.
* Daily events.
